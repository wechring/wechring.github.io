About

Where code meets behavior

I'm Wech Ring, a software engineer in Baltimore with an unusual pairing of degrees from Johns Hopkins University: Computer Science and Behavioral Biology. That combination isn't an accident — I'm most at home building software that helps us understand people.

As a Research Software Engineer at Johns Hopkins, I built and shipped a Python web platform that runs multi-condition online experiments end to end — randomization, real-time data validation, and messaging — and scaled it to over 1,000 study participants. Before that, I spent a summer at Yale's Interactive Machines Group engineering systems that modulated AI agent behavior in real time for studies on human trust in machines.

Outside research, I build for the web: a startup collaboration platform in Next.js and Firebase, an interactive timeline spanning 400+ years of historical data, and an AI-powered trading assistant. As an executive board member of the National Society of Black Engineers, I organize workshops and events that expand representation of Black engineers in technology.

I care about clean architecture, interfaces that respect the people using them, and data you can trust. Currently focused on full-stack software engineering roles.
